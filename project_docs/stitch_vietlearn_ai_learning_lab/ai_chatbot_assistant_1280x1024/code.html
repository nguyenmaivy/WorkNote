Can you explain the formula for the Taylor Series expansion on page 12 of my notes? I don't understand how the factorial part works.

smart_toy

Certainly! Based on Page 12 of Advanced Calculus Notes, the Taylor series expansion of a function f(x) about a point a is given by:

f(x) = f(a) + f'(a)(x-a) + (f''(a)/2!)(x-a)² + ... + (fⁿ(a)/n!)(x-a)ⁿ

The factorial part (n!) in the denominator is crucial. It accounts for the repeated differentiation.

  • When you differentiate xⁿ, you get nxⁿ⁻¹.
  • Differentiate again, you get n(n-1)xⁿ⁻².
  • By the nth derivative, you've multiplied by n * (n-1) * (n-2) * ... * 1, which is exactly n!.

Dividing by n! cancels out this growing coefficient so that the nth derivative of the polynomial exactly matches the nth derivative of the original function f(x) at point a.
